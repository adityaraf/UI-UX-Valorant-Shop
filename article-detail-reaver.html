Reaver 3.0 Collection: Everything We Know So Far

Alex Rodriguez Senior Valorant Analyst

The Reaver collection has been one of Valorant's most iconic and beloved skin lines since the game's beta. After the massive success of Reaver 2.0, rumors have been circulating about a third installment in this dark, edgy collection. Here's everything we know so far about the potential Reaver 3.0 collection.

The Legacy of Reaver

The original Reaver collection was introduced during Valorant's beta phase and quickly became a fan favorite with its dark aesthetic and distinctive sound effects. The collection was temporarily removed after the beta but returned in November 2020 with the Vandal, Operator, Sheriff, Guardian, and knife.

Reaver 2.0 launched in August 2022, bringing the same gothic design to the Phantom, Odin, Spectre, Ghost, and a new karambit knife. Both collections were massive commercial successes for Riot Games, cementing Reaver as one of the most popular skin lines in the game.

Leaked Information

According to several reliable leakers in the Valorant community, Reaver 3.0 is currently in development and might be released in the third quarter of 2025. While Riot Games has not officially confirmed this information, data miners have found references to "RVR3" in recent game updates.

The most substantial leak came from prominent Valorant leaker @ValorLeaks, who shared a cryptic image on social media showing what appears to be a Reaver-styled Bulldog with the caption "The darkness returns. #Reaver3".

Potential Weapons

Based on the existing Reaver collections and the weapons that haven't yet received the Reaver treatment, we can speculate that Reaver 3.0 might include:

  • Bulldog - Strongly suggested by leaks
  • Marshal - One of the few rifles without a Reaver variant
  • Ares - Would complete the heavy weapon category alongside the Odin
  • Shorty - The only sidearm without a Reaver skin
  • Judge - Would be the first shotgun in the Reaver collection
  • Melee Weapon - Possibly a sword or axe design based on community requests

Expected Features

If Reaver 3.0 follows the pattern of previous collections, we can expect:

  • Color Variants - The traditional red, white, and black variants, plus possibly a new exclusive color
  • Finisher - A new or updated version of the soul-harvesting finisher effect
  • Animations - Unique reload and equip animations consistent with the Reaver theme
  • Sound Effects - The distinctive hollow, echoing sound that Reaver skins are known for
  • VFX - Purple/red magical effects on kills and possibly during reload animations

Price Point

Based on previous Reaver collections, we can expect Reaver 3.0 to be priced at the Premium tier (1775 VP per weapon). The bundle would likely cost around 7100 VP, offering a discount compared to buying each skin individually.

If Riot decides to include special features beyond what previous Reaver collections offered, there's a possibility the price could increase to the Exclusive tier (2175 VP per weapon).

Community Reaction

The Valorant community has been overwhelmingly positive about the prospect of a third Reaver collection. A recent poll on the Valorant subreddit showed that 78% of players would be "very likely" or "definitely" purchase at least one skin from a potential Reaver 3.0 collection.

Many players have been creating concept art and suggestions for what they'd like to see in the new collection, with particular emphasis on a Reaver Judge and a more elaborate melee weapon.

"The Reaver collection represents the perfect balance of edgy aesthetics, satisfying sound design, and clean visuals that don't distract during gameplay. It's no wonder players are clamoring for a third installment." â€” Sarah Chen, Valorant Skin Analyst

Expected Release Date

While there's no official confirmation, the consensus among leakers is that Reaver 3.0 could be released sometime between July and September 2025. This would align with Riot's typical schedule of releasing major skin collections every few months.

Some speculate that the collection might be timed to coincide with Halloween 2025, given the dark and gothic theme of the Reaver line.

Conclusion

While much of this information is based on leaks and speculation, the evidence suggests that Reaver 3.0 is indeed in development. Given the immense popularity of the previous Reaver collections, a third installment would be a logical step for Riot Games.

We'll continue to update this article as more information becomes available. In the meantime, Valorant players might want to start saving their VP for what could be one of the most anticipated skin collections of 2025.
